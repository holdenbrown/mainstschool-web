Early Childhood Program Overview

Age Range
3 years old and up
Class Size
10-12 students

Our early childhood classroom welcomes children starting at age 3. It is comprised of what is typically considered the preschool and pre-Kindergarten years. Students are required to be potty independent before starting. We accept enrollment any time during the year as long as there are openings.

During this three-year span, children have an innate power to teach themselves. Our early childhood classroom is set up to allow them to explore their interests and dive into their learning in an independent manner. Children are also highly physically motivated at this stage and movement is not just allowed but encouraged.

Our Teaching Philosophy

Natural Development & Independence

The varied age groupings allow each student the natural evolution of being the youngest and learning to ask for help from their more experienced peers to becoming the capable and respectful leaders of the class.

As students prepare to transition, they may spend part of their day in the elementary classroom, creating smooth transitions between programs.

This classroom typically includes 10-12 members allowing the teacher to really know their students well.

Movement & Exploration

Children are highly physically motivated at this stage and movement is not just allowed but encouraged. Our classroom is designed to support active learning and exploration.

After touring, a classroom visit is recommended if possible to allow the child to meet the teacher and interact briefly in the environment.

This hands-on approach helps children develop both physically and cognitively in a supportive environment.

What Our Day Looks Like

Morning Routine
  • Arrival: We gather in our backyard for playtime before the start of our workday.
  • Morning Work: Put away belongings, remove shoes, and begin morning work.
  • Morning Meeting: Pledge of allegiance, calendar math, schedule discussion, classroom jobs, music and movement, and literacy lesson.
Learning Centers
  • Centers: Brief introduction and planning time, then students move throughout centers at their own pace.
  • Individual Work: Time for individual work with the teacher.
  • Snack: Family-style snack while reviewing center work.
Academic Focus
  • Writing Journals: Write, draw, or color in journals to reflect on thoughts and practice literacy and fine motor skills.
  • Circle Time: Group meeting for math activities.
  • Science: Classroom discussion, exploration, and experimentation.
Enrichment & Play
  • Recess: Develop gross motor skills, dramatic play, and outdoor time.
  • Specials: Art, Music, Library, PE
  • Story Time: Student or teacher-chosen books with discussion and creative adaptations.
