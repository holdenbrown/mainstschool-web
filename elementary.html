Our Teaching Philosophy

Fostering Lifelong Learners

We believe that children should be curious, creative, joyful learners that carry this throughout their lives. Our goal is to model the amazement of discovery and the payoff of perseverance.

Kindness and compassion are critical to becoming good people who do great things in the world. Our classroom is a safe space to learn and grow together.

Our elementary program is comprised of early elementary and upper elementary with curriculum that is individualized to each student's needs. We do not practice 'one size fits all' learning, believing that each student's needs are different and must be differently addressed.

This philosophy places emphasis on the students' role in the learning experience, allowing them to engage at their own level and in their own time while embracing the challenge of meeting their individual goals.

Workshop-Based Learning

Morning Meeting
  • Connection Time: Students and teacher connect before diving into the day.
  • Share & Listen: Opportunities to share news about our lives and get to know each other better.
  • Planning Together: Make plans for upcoming events and set intentions for the day.
Literacy Workshops
  • Mini-Lessons: Direct instruction with read-alouds and examples for all grade levels.
  • Guided Practice: Individual and small group work with the teacher.
  • Independent Reading & Writing: Students engage in authentic text experiences.
Math Workshop
  • Math Talks: Number sense and relationship discussions with mental math.
  • Hands-On Activities: Math games and challenging puzzles to develop fluency.
  • Individual Practice: "Must-do" and "can-do" work tailored to each student's skills.
Project-Based Learning
  • Artful Learning: Arts integration to spark curiosity and discovery.
  • Research & Create: Purposeful investigation and creative expression.
  • Cross-Curricular: Projects weave into workshop times with complementary books and writing.
What is Workshop-Based Learning?

A workshop is comprised of a mini-lesson that applies to all grade levels through the specific skill that it's teaching. Students then practice at their own individual levels, and have opportunities to participate in one-on-one practice with the teacher, ensuring personalized instruction that meets each child where they are.
