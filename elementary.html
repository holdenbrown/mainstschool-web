Elementary Program Overview

Grade Levels
Kindergarten through 5th Grade
Learning Model
Project-Based Learning

Our elementary program at Main Street School nurtures the natural curiosity and creativity that children are born with, creating a foundation for lifelong learning through hands-on exploration and meaningful project work.

Our elementary program is broken down into lower elementary (Kindergarten through 2nd grade) and upper elementary (3rd through 5th grade), allowing us to tailor our approach to the developmental needs of each age group. This structure enables younger students to build fundamental skills while older students develop greater independence and deeper critical thinking abilities.

Our Teaching Philosophy

Creating Lifelong Learners

Our goal is to model the amazement of discovery and the payoff of perseverance. Kids are all born curious asking insighful questions about the world around them. We view the loss of curiosity as a great tragedy and its our mission to instead bolsters a young learners curiosity.

We nnurture curiosity through creative expression as students design and accomplish personally meaningful projects.

Our teaching philosophy places emphasis on the students' role in the learning experience, allowing them to engage at their own level and in their own time while embracing the challenge of meeting their individual goals.

What is Project-Based Learning?

Project-based learning integrates multiple subjects and skills into meaningful, hands-on assignments.

The goal of project based learning is to bring together interdisciplinary skills like math, science, art, and, english, into one project enabling the student to grow in numerous skills simultaneously.

This approach teaches students to process and integrate diverse information from multiple sources, developing critical thinking and problem-solving skills that prepare them for real-world challenges.

Our Learning Philosophy in Action

Class Meeting
  • Connection Time: Students and the teacher share news about their lives.
  • Share & Listen: Opportunities to share news about our lives and get to know each other better.
  • Planning Together: Make plans for the day and upcoming events and set intentions for the day.
Math Workshop
  • Math Talks: Building intuition behind the concept of numbers through discussion of math problems that don't have just one answer.
  • Practice: Students practice with the teacher either indivually or in small groups based ability and need.
  • Time Management: A mix of required and optional assignments gives students a sense of ownership over their learning encouraging autonomy and intrinsic motivation.
  • Hands-On Activities: Students build math skills through puzzles and games, choosing to work independently or with peers.
Literacy Workshops
  • Mini-Lessons: The lesson is designed to apply to all grades and is given first with instructions, then supproted with examples.
  • Guided Practice: Individual and small group work with the teacher.
  • Independent Reading & Writing: Students choose what assignment or skill to work on, usually engaging in reading or writing, allowing them to take the wheel and steer in the direction of curiosity.
Project-Based Learning
  • Creative Problem Solving: Students solve problems creatively through meaningful hands-on work.
  • Research & Create: Purposeful investigation and creative expression.
  • Cross-Curricular: Projects weave into workshop times with complementary books and writing.
Safe Learning Environment
  • Kindness & Compassion: Critical values for becoming good people who do great things.
  • Safe Space: Classroom environment where students can learn and grow together.
  • Individual Support: One-on-one and small group instruction tailored to needs.
