High School Program Overview

Grade Levels
9th through 12th Grade
Graduation
Meeting Iowa State Requirements

Our curriculum in high school becomes more diversified with more student choice. Every student must meet the requirements established by the State of Iowa for graduation, but those requirements can be met by a variety of courses and in an order conducive to each student's learning needs. Therefore, a freshman may enroll in a class that a junior is also taking.

Our high school curriculum is developed with high standards embracing the belief that all students will assimilate the content in each course depending on their academic level, skills, and aptitude. We believe every student will "fill up their knowledge banks" as they are willing and able.

Our Teaching Philosophy

Critical Thinking & Investigation

Through investigation, discussions, debates, and individual projects, students are encouraged to synthesize knowledge and use critical thinking and questioning to evaluate events in history, analyze literature and share points of view, or investigate science phenomena.

Our goal is to help our students understand the world in which they live and prepare them to be active and informed citizens of their communities.

It is an environment where everyone is welcome and all points of view and opinions are considered valuable.

Student Choice & Flexibility

We offer our students a variety of classes in each discipline in order to meet their graduation requirements and at the same time, interests.

Projects can be devised to meet the interests and abilities of the students and discussions are centered around sharing knowledge and encouraging critical thinking practices.

This flexible approach allows students to pursue their passions while meeting all academic requirements for graduation.

Currently Offered Classes

  • Comparative Political Systems: investigates and compares/contrasts political systems from around the world 1 semester
  • U.S. Government: investigates the development, philosophy and intricacies of our own government 1 semester
  • Contemporary World Issues: includes discussions and research on issues like terrorism, refugees, democracies in crisis, human rights, and the evolution of AI 1 semester
  • U.S. History: investigates the causes/effects of the early settlement of the United States, its expansion, revolutions and wars, and the development of the American character and values 2 semesters
  • World History: explores how different societies, countries, and cultures around the world have developed and interacted, influenced each other, and become increasingly interconnected throughout history 2 semesters
  • 3 Powers: investigates the history and development of Russia, China and the United States as major players on the world stage and how they have impacted each other 1 semester

  • English 9–12: includes reading both fiction and non fiction novels and short stories/articles, building writing skills through all forms of composition (narrative, analysis, persuasive, informative, etc.) and acquiring skills of analysis of literature, actively seeking feedback from others in order to gain insights into their own thinking, and developing communication skills. 2 semesters
  • Multi-Cultural Literature offers two separate semesters:
    • One focusing on Native American writing and Asian-American pieces.
    • The second studies Hispanic literature and African-American novels and stories.
  • Short Stories: surveys a variety of short stories and asks students to participate in analysis, discussions and writing of said stories. 1 semester
  • The World of Ray Bradbury: focuses entirely on the short stories and novels of Ray Brandbury. 1 semester
  • Creative Writing: allows students to explore their own talents in writing short stories, poetry, music or other forms of self-expression. 1 semester
  • Speech and Debate: requires students to participate in debates focused on topics of their choice as well as topics of relevance to the world in which we live. Students will also practice public speaking, delivering a variety of kinds of speeches from introductions to storytelling to speeches to inspire. 1 semester
