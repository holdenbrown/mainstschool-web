Program Overview

At the high school level, the curriculum becomes more diversified with more student choice. Every student must meet the requirements established by the State of Iowa for graduation, but those requirements can be met by a variety of courses and in an order conducive to each student's learning needs.

Therefore, a freshman may enroll in a class that a junior is also taking. Our high school curriculum encourages students to synthesize knowledge and use insight and creativity to better understand the world in which they live.

Academic Disciplines

History & Social Studies
  • Political Systems: Investigation and comparison of political systems from around the world.
  • Government: Study of the intricacies of our own government.
  • Revolution & War: Analysis of causes and effects of revolution and war.
  • Citizenship: Preparation to be active and informed citizens.
English & Communication
  • Group Discussion: Various forms of group discussion and individual research.
  • Self-Motivation: Students encouraged to start tasks without prompting.
  • Writing Skills: All forms of composition (narrative, analysis, persuasive, informative).
  • Literature Analysis: Active seeking of feedback to gain insights.

Science & Mathematics

Science Curriculum
  • Problem Solving: Identify, formulate and solve problems using appropriate information.
  • Critical Thinking: Apply analytical skills to solve scientific data sets.
  • Hypothesis Testing: Test hypotheses and draw inferences using quantitative analysis.
  • Data Analysis: Develop skills in interpreting and analyzing scientific data.
Mathematics Approach
  • Mixed Levels: Classes comprised of all levels 9th-12th grade.
  • Individual Focus: Math tends to be individual or very small-group based.
  • Level-Based: Instruction based on the level and class choice.
  • Personalized Learning: Each student's mathematical journey is unique.

Learning Philosophy

Our classroom learning experience is based on Bloom's Taxonomy which includes knowledge, comprehension, application, analysis, synthesis, and evaluation in an ascending order of cognitive complexity where evaluation represents the highest level.

Cognitive Development: Progressive complexity in thinking skills
Creative Synthesis: Combining knowledge in innovative ways
Critical Analysis: Deep examination of information and concepts
Evaluation Skills: Highest level of cognitive processing
