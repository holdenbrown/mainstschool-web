Middle School Program Overview

Grade Levels
6th through 8th Grade
Learning Model
Project-Based Learning

Our middle school program at Main Street serves as a time for students to examine their strengths and weaknesses, set learning goals and acquire new skills as they prepare for the eventual transition to high school level.

Our middle school is comprised of 6th through 8th graders who participate in a mix of large groups, small leveled groups and individual project work. With the range of ages and maturity, our 6th graders look up to the older students who "know the ropes", while the 8th graders in the classroom organically learn about mentoring, leadership, and collaboration.

Our Teaching Philosophy

Goal Setting & Skill Development

Students examine their strengths and weaknesses, set learning goals and acquire new skills as they prepare for the eventual transition to high school level.

With the range of ages and maturity, our 6th graders look up to the older students who "know the ropes", while the 8th graders organically learn about mentoring, leadership, and collaboration.

This creates a natural progression where students develop both academically and socially in a supportive peer environment.

Project-Based Learning

We consider ourselves a project-based school. Projects can be long, multiple-year endeavors or a short couple of weeks, connecting as many curricular areas as possible.

The projects aim to make the skills or information we are studying meaningful and relevant to the students' lives and future goals.

This approach helps students see connections between subjects and understand the real-world applications of their learning.

Learning Structure in Action

Large Group Activities
  • "What's in the News": Students discuss current events and contemporary issues.
  • Social Development: Reading and discussing articles related to social behavior.
  • Self-Reflection: Engaging in activities that promote personal growth.
Small Group Learning
  • Academic Disciplines: Students engage at their own academic levels.
  • History Topics: WWI and WWII taught at different depths of inquiry.
  • Mathematics: Small group or individual instruction based on level.
Project Examples
  • Short-Term: WWII leaders "twitter-wall" - what would they have tweeted?
  • Long-Term: "Collecting Community Histories" - students become historians and reporters.
  • Technology: Audio production and StoryCorps-style interviews.
Enrichment & Exploration
  • Specialty Teachers: Weekly instruction in various creative disciplines.
  • Weekly Outings: Community and nature exploration when weather permits.
  • Creative Development: Time devoted to developing creativity and exploration.

Featured Project: Community Histories

Iowa Public Radio's Partnership with Young Journalists

Our students have become historians and reporters as they interview and record community members' histories. They have learned the technology and skills to produce audio stories StoryCorps style, building their public speaking and interviewing skills.

They are learning that everyone has a story to share and history isn't limited to the history texts. Each of us will write our own history. This ongoing project gives them a sense of accomplishment and belonging in our community.
